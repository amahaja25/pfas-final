Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, also known as PFAS or "forever chemicals," are a group of manmade chemicals found in consumer, commercial and industrial products, according to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. Trace amounts of these substances can be found in people, animals, water, soil, air and food across the world because components of these chemicals break down incredibly slowly and stay in the environment.
